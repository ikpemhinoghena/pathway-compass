Common Registration Issues

Quick answers to frequently asked registration questions.

I can't log in to the new portal

The new portal requires your school email ending with @byupathway.edu, not just your Church account. You also need to use this link portal.byupathway.edu to log in. Check your email for a message titled "New BYU-Pathway Portal" and get your new school email address.

Steps to resolve:

  1. 1. Search your email for "New BYU-Pathway Portal"
  2. 2. Find your username ending in @byupathway.edu
  3. 3. Go to portal.byupathway.edu
  4. 4. Sign in with your Church account if prompted
Still not finding your email? Contact Support

When is the registration deadline?

Applications are open year-round! Courses start every 7–8 weeks. If you miss the deadline for the current block, you can simply register for the next available block.

Check Academic Calendar Check Application Deadlines

I'm getting a "hold" message when trying to register

Holds are restrictions placed on your account that prevent registration. They must be resolved before you can add courses. Common causes:

Financial Hold

Past due balance. Pay via the portal to clear.

Endorsement Hold

Ecclesiastical endorsement needed.

Gathering / Institute Hold

Must register for a gathering or institute group.

Watch how to clear hold

Which path should I choose: PathwayConnect or Advanced?

PathwayConnect (Recommended)

  • • First three semesters include foundational courses
  • • No ACT/SAT required for admission
  • • All courses count toward your degree
  • • Built-in academic and religion courses
  • • Ideal for those returning to education after a break or looking for a structured transition into university-level study

Advanced Path

  • • For students with significant university experience
  • • Primarily for U.S. students
  • • Requires 2.0+ GPA or 3.0 in PathwayConnect
  • • More detailed application process
  • • Direct entry to degree program

Member Record Number (MRN)

The Member Record Number (MRN) is only applicable to members of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints.

Non-members can skip this field. It's optional during registration.

If you're a member and don't know your MRN, contact your local Leader or Unit Clerk.

Why the English Assessment?

To succeed in BYU-Pathway's university-level courses, students need a specific level of English proficiency. This assessment ensures you can confidently understand course materials, participate in discussions, and complete assignments.

No "Pass" or "Fail"

You cannot fail this test! Based on your results:

  • You'll either be admitted directly to PathwayConnect.
  • Or you'll start in EnglishConnect to improve your skills until you're ready.

Either way, you are starting your journey toward a degree!
